Abstract

Accurate 3D Facial Landmark Detection (3DFLD) is a fundamental component of many 3D face analysis pipelines, yet existing methods largely rely on pre-aligned inputs and degrade severely even under mild 3D rotations. This limitation stems from the lack of rigid motion awareness in current architectures: these assume a canonical pose and therefore require pose normalization as a preprocessing step, while reliable pose normalization itself depends on accurate landmark localization, thus creating a circular dependency between landmark detection and pose normalization. In this work, we propose SE3-3DFL, a fully SE(3)-equivariant UNet-like architecure for 3D facial landmark detection directly from raw point clouds. By design, through the adoption of steerable methods, SE3-3DFL produces landmark predictions that transform coherently with the same roto-translation (SE(3) group) applied to the input, eliminating the need for prior alignment. Extensive experiments show that the proposed model achieves competitive results if compared to state-of-the-art 3DFLD models on canonically aligned data while significantly outperforming existing methods under arbitrary rigid transformations.

Results

FLD performance is reported in terms of MAE and MAPE between predicted and ground-truth landmarks. Ablation Studies

SE3-3DFL under the Test rand 0–360 protocol. Local relies on fixed k-NN aggregation, Local-Att adds attention over local neighborhoods, and Stratified further incorporates sparse global context via stratified sampling. Results across Neutral, Emotion, and Expression show progressively improved performance, with Stratified achieving the lowest error.
